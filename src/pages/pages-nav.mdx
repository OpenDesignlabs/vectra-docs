# Pages & Navigation

Every Vectra project can have multiple pages. Each page has its own canvas, its own URL, and its own content.

## Adding a page

Open the **Pages panel** by clicking the pages icon in the left toolbar. Click **+ New Page**. Give it a name — Vectra automatically creates a URL slug from the name.

| Page name | Auto-generated slug |
|---|---|
| Home | `/` |
| About Us | `/about-us` |
| Contact | `/contact` |
| Blog | `/blog` |

You can rename the slug at any time by clicking on it in the Pages panel.

## Switching between pages

Click any page in the Pages panel to switch the canvas to that page. Each page is completely independent — elements on one page don't affect another.

## Deleting a page

Right-click a page in the Pages panel and choose **Delete**. You can't delete the last remaining page in a project.

## Reordering pages

Drag pages up and down in the Pages panel to reorder them. The order affects the navigation component if you have one.

## Navigation between pages

When your project has two or more pages, Vectra automatically generates a `Navbar` component when you export. It links to all your pages using the slugs you defined.

<Callout type="tip">
  If you want a custom navigation, add a **Container** element to your page, place text or button elements inside it, and give each one a link property pointing to your page slugs.
</Callout>

## SEO settings per page

Click the settings icon next to any page name in the Pages panel to open its SEO settings. You can set:

- **Page title** — what appears in the browser tab and Google results
- **Description** — the snippet shown in search results
- **OG image** — the preview image when shared on social media
