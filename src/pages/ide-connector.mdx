# IDE Connector (MCP)

Vectra's IDE Connector allows you to link the Studio directly to your favorite developer tools like **Cursor**, **VS Code**, or **Claude Desktop**. This enables your local AI agent to "see" the live canvas and perform edits through natural language commands in your own IDE.

## Getting Started

1. **Open the MCP Panel:** Click the CPU icon `⚙️` in the left sidebar of the Studio.
2. **Select your IDE:** Choose between Cursor, VS Code, Windsurf, or Claude Desktop.
3. **Copy the Configuration:** Vectra provides a custom JSON configuration block specifically for your project.
4. **Paste and Restart:** Paste the config into your IDE's settings (as instructed in the panel) and restart your IDE.

## The Local Bridge

We recommend using the **Local Bridge** method for the best experience. It doesn't require a public URL and works completely offline.

- **Zero configuration:** Once the bridge is installed via the provided `npx` command, it works instantly.
- **Fast:** Mutations happen with sub-millisecond latency as the bridge talks directly to the browser's WebContainer.
- **Secure:** Your project data never leaves your machine.

---

## Commands and Tools

Your IDE agent has access to several specialized tools:
- `vectra_get_project`: List all pages and theme tokens.
- `vectra_add_element`: Insert headers, text, or containers.
- `vectra_update_element`: Modify any property or Tailwind class on a specific node.
- `vectra_run_ai`: Trigger the Studio's internal AI generation from your IDE.
